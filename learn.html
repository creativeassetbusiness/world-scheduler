How it works

The concepts behind the World Scheduler — FAQ, documentation, and the philosophy of engineering civilization as a system.

What the World Scheduler does

The World Scheduler is a system for measuring civilization using physical numbers: calories, water, energy, housing, medicine, materials, labor hours, and transport. It uses safety margins and production data to find real shortages and set real priorities.

Needs → Supply → Shortage

Calculate what people actually need, then compare that need to what is available. When supply is lower than the safety-target, the system finds the shortage in exact numbers.

Tax direction becomes action

When a resource is short, direction is clear: build farms, water systems, greenhouses, factories, transport, or automation.

Labor hours reveal true cost

Goods require production. Production requires labor. Robots lower human labor and free people for higher quality of life.

The philosophy behind the scheduler

This is not redistribution based on politics. It is engineering civilization as a system. You calculate need, add a safety margin, measure supply, find the gap, direct resources, and repeat.

  • Do we have enough calories?
  • Do we have enough water?
  • Do we have enough housing?
  • Do we have enough energy?
  • Do we have enough medicine?
  • Do we have enough materials?
  • Do we have enough production capacity?
  • Do we have enough transportation?

FAQ

How does family quota work?

The scheduler uses family size, regional needs, health, and activity to build a life plan. Premium quota is possible, but the system flags anything above a normal allowance so the extra is clearly visible.

This is where the world scheduler turns raw numbers into a simple life plan: food, housing, energy, medicine, transport, and luxury.

What is the Country of Earth?

The Country of Earth is a designation earned by the nation that achieves the highest measurable public-data transparency. Only publicly verifiable data counts. The closer a country gets to 100% trusted self-description across coverage, recency, consistency, and machine readability, the closer it gets to being crowned.

What is the Margin of Error?

The Margin of Error is a visible recognition system. When someone is directly affected by a resource shortage — food, water, housing, energy, medicine, or materials — they can claim impact. The system scores their exposure and issues a public badge. This turns invisible sacrifice into visible stewardship data.

What does the World Engine do?

The World Engine is a spatial planning model rendered in 3D. It organizes the planet into regions, zones, buildings, rooms, windows, panels, surfaces, and nano-level primitives (faces, edges, vertices). Each layer carries metrics. Each primitive is inspectable. The engine bridges macro policy to micro material accounting.

How is this funded?

Donations launch the system. Paid timed sessions build the operating surplus that keeps World Scheduler and its ground-device fleet alive indefinitely. The target is a 100-year endowment runway strong enough to fund operations and 1,000,000 embedded field devices forever after that horizon.

About World Scheduler

World Scheduler was built to answer one question: can we run civilization by the numbers?

Not by politics. Not by markets alone. By measuring physical reality — calories, water, housing, energy, medicine, materials — and comparing need to supply with a safety margin. Where there's a gap, there's a priority. Where there's surplus, there's room to grow.

The first national planning campus will anchor in South Dakota or Colorado, giving every citizen a practical path to balanced infrastructure, resilient food systems, and fair distribution. One campus. One thousand years. One ledger that makes sacrifice visible.
